INFO 333 · UX Design · Spring 2025

Team Project · Figma · Accessibility · UIUC

I Map.

A multi-function campus map concept integrating accessibility routing, real-time space occupancy, dining options, and sports schedules into one unified platform — reducing the friction of navigating UIUC's large campus.

UX DesignFigmaWireframingAccessibility (WCAG)User PersonaINFO 333

What I did

Built interactive wireframes and conducted annotated walkthroughs to evaluate information hierarchy and navigation patterns. Developed a user persona in Figma, mapped pain points and motivations, and iterated on interface layouts based on peer usability feedback.

Key outcome

Presented a final concept addressing WCAG accessibility needs, schedule integration, and real-time space availability — balancing four distinct user modes within a single coherent navigation system.

IS 101 · Game Studies & Design · Fall 2024

Co-designed · iSchool Spring Showcase · UIUC

The Other Side of Life.

A social-awareness board game exploring racial bias and systemic inequality — inspired by The Game of Life, but redesigned so different characters interact with the same events in fundamentally different ways. Players gradually realise the bias baked into the system.

Game DesignSocial AwarenessPlaytestingAlgorithmic BiasIS 101iSchool Showcase

Problem I solved

Identified a core pacing problem — too many blank board squares were causing gameplay to stall. Proposed and tested rule revisions that significantly improved engagement and made the bias mechanics more legible to players.

Process & reflection

Co-designed with a team of 5. Led two rounds of playtesting, documented player confusion, and iterated on card design. Presented at the iSchool Spring Showcase.

IS 226 · Interaction Design · Spring 2025

Mobile App Concept · Paper Wireframes · Interface Analysis

MailMind.

A mobile app concept that automatically categorises student emails — assignments, events, packages, alerts — using keyword detection and contextual tagging. Designed to reduce the mental load of inbox management for busy students.

Interaction DesignMobile UXWireframingVisual HierarchyIS 226

What I designed

Created paper wireframes and interaction flows for login and inbox screens. Annotated key UI decisions — auto-labelled tags, contextual sidebar generation, chunked email categories — grounding each choice in principles of visual hierarchy, chunking, and alignment.

Interface analysis

Analysed five real-world interfaces (Lookback, Best Buy, eBay, StockX, Instagram) through visual remix exercises — extracting patterns around information density, progressive disclosure, and CTA placement.
